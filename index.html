About StudySync

Helping students schedule smarter, together.

🎯

Our Mission

StudySync was built to solve a problem every student faces - finding a time that works for everyone. No more endless group chats trying to coordinate schedules.

🧠

Smart Scheduling

Our CSP-based algorithm analyses everyone's availability and finds the optimal meeting times, taking into account travel times and member locations.

📅

Calendar Integration

Connect your Google Calendar and let StudySync automatically import your availability - no manual input needed.

📍

Travel Aware

StudySync calculates real travel times for each member so everyone knows when to leave and can plan their journey in advance.

The Story

StudySync started as a final year Computer Science project, born out of frustration with the chaos of organising group study sessions. What began as a simple scheduling tool grew into a full-stack platform with smart algorithms, calendar sync, and real-time travel calculations.

Built with Flask, PostgreSQL, and a JavaScript frontend, StudySync is designed to be fast, intuitive, and genuinely useful for students.

FAQ

Is StudySync free to use? ▼
Yes! StudySync is completely free for all students.
How does the scheduling algorithm work? ▼
We use a Constraint Satisfaction Problem (CSP) algorithm that finds time slots where the maximum number of members are available, factoring in travel time buffers.
Is my Google Calendar data safe? ▼
Yes. We only read your busy/free times and never store or share your calendar event details.
How many members can a group have? ▼
There's no hard limit. StudySync works best with groups of 2 to 10 members.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

1. Who We Are

StudySync is a web-based scheduling application developed as an academic project at the University of Greenwich. The data controller for personal data processed through this service is the project author, Amine El-hamri.

2. What Data We Collect

We collect the following personal data when you use StudySync:

  • Full name and email address (provided at registration)
  • Password (stored as a bcrypt hash — your plaintext password is never stored)
  • Profile picture (optional, stored in Google Cloud Storage)
  • Home and work/university address (optional, used for travel time calculations)
  • Availability data (time slots you mark as free within study groups)
  • Google Calendar access tokens (only if you choose to connect your Google Calendar)

3. Why We Collect It

All data collected is used solely to provide the StudySync scheduling service. Email addresses are used for login and meeting notifications. Location data is used only to calculate travel times to meeting venues. Calendar tokens are used only to import your availability when you request it. No data is sold, shared with third parties for marketing, or used for any purpose other than operating the service.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018, our lawful basis for processing your personal data is your consent. By registering for an account, you consent to your data being stored and processed as described in this policy. You may withdraw consent at any time by deleting your account.

5. Data Retention

Your personal data is retained for as long as your account remains active. If you delete your account, all associated data — including your profile, availability records, group memberships, and meeting history — is permanently removed from our systems within 24 hours. Google Calendar access tokens are deleted immediately upon account deletion. Profile pictures stored in Google Cloud Storage are removed within 7 days of account deletion.

6. Where Your Data Is Stored

Your data is stored on Google Cloud infrastructure (Cloud SQL and Cloud Storage) hosted in the EU West 2 region (London). Google LLC acts as a data processor under a Data Processing Agreement. Google's participation in the UK-US data bridge framework provides the legal basis for any incidental data transfers.

7. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — you can request a copy of your data
  • Right to rectification — you can update your name and email in your profile settings
  • Right to erasure — you can permanently delete your account and all associated data from your profile page
  • Right to object — you can stop using the service at any time
  • Right to withdraw consent — deleting your account withdraws all consent

8. Security

All communication between your browser and our servers is encrypted using HTTPS. Passwords are stored as bcrypt hashes and are never accessible in plaintext. Google Calendar access uses OAuth 2.0, meaning we never handle your Google password directly.

9. Contact

For any privacy-related questions or to exercise your data rights, please contact us via the Contact page.
